Jhoan is a 5 year old boy who suffered severe brain injury secondary to a traumatic delivery. He has the incurable diagnosis of cerebral palsy with mental retardation. Despite the emotional/physical challenges and scarce family resources that Jhoan requires, his parents adore him and struggle to care for him in the most dignified manner possible.

When I met Jhoan in May, 2005, he was severely malnourished. Even on his best days, feeding Jhoan was problematic due to the difficulty he has swallowing. He had recently developed a prolonged gastroenteritis and maintaining his nutrition had become impossible for the family. He was rapidly losing critical weight and it was apparent that he wouldn’t survive without a major intervention.

Utilizing the professional services of an inpatient renourishment program located an hour from Montero, the RVF made possible a three month inpatient stay in the San Carlos Refeeding Centre. Jhoan’s mother visited the Centre weekly to learn how to prepare nutritious, protein-rich yet affordable meals for Jhoan. This skill of course benefits not only Jhoan, but the entire family.

 

RVF Contribution:  $30 (included all costs associated with 90 days of inpatient care).  Ongoing nutritional supplementation, frequent weight checks & education are provided by the Community Health Worker Program (also supported by the RVF).

RVF Cost:  100% of initial evaluation ($50).  Thereafter, 50-75% of all costs for daily anti-seizure medications (~$5/wk.) & evaluation by neurologist/lab work every 4-6 months ($8 per visit)

Family Contribution:  25-50% of all costs depending on ability

**Note:  it is uncommon for a Bolivian child with a severe chronic disease to receive such a complete intervention. The Nova family wishes to express their tremendous gratitude for the opportunity that the RVF donors offered to their son and family.**
