Liliana has permanent cochlear damage secondary to chronic, untreated ear infections.  The cochlea are sensitive structures in the inner ear that are integral to our ability to interpret sound.  Therefore, Liliana has what is referred to as ‘acquired deafness’.

When I met Liliana at age eleven in May, 2005, she was indeed attending school.  However, as she could no longer hear and hadn’t been taught sign language, the actual value of her education was of course very limited. Her pleasant, cooperative demeanor allowed her to simply pass through classes -- without the specialized language instruction and interaction that she requires.

The RVF is assisting Liliana to now attend the ‘Santa Lucia School for Special Education’.  She is adored by her instructors, is enthusiastically learning and communicating with formal sign language skills, and is sharing in friendships for the first time in years.
