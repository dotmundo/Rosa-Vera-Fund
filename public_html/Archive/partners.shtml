Curamericas is a nonprofit organization headquartered in Raleigh, North
Carolina, that provides primary care and practical health education to women
and children living in poverty throughout Latin America.

Curamericas provides the RVF with tax-exempt status, third-party donation
collection and assists in fund administration.

El Consejo de Salud Rural Andino (CSRA), or The Rural Andean Health
Counsel, is a Bolivian nonprofit organization that has worked to reduce the
morbidity/mortality of poverty-stricken communities throughout Bolivia.

CSRA oversees the selection of RVF beneficiaries, administration of all
medical/social services, and official fund accounting/reporting.

CSRA and Curamericas have worked
together in Montero since 1983 to provide:
  • child survival techniques
  • maternal/ reproductive health
  • micro-enterprise development
  • water sanitation
  • tuberculosis intervention

Collaborations include:
  • The US Agency for International Development
  • The Center for Disease Control and Prevention
  • CARE International
  • Freedom From Hunger
  • Water for People

These efforts have established some of the region's largest
and most successful health care initiatives
