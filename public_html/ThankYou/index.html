October 14, 2012
Dear Friends,

Just a note to thank you for coming out last weekend to help us celebrate another successful year for the Rosa Vera Fund.  With the start of a beautiful fall, it seemed like a great time to reach out to our friends and supporters and say a genuine "Thank You" for your support.

Some of the year's highlights are:

  • Juan is receiving physical therapy to help him recover from brain injuries he received after he tragically fell from a motorcycle taxi.
  • We are 1/2 way to raising the funding needed for a specialized hearing aid implant for 12 year-old Maria Ciela, an orphan who we've been supporting for the past five years. Maria's hearing loss is complicated, but correctable. She is so excited that she is close to gaining the ability to hear!
  • Hugo Nelson made it!  A few years ago, Hugo was completely blind due to a condition called trichiasis, which causes the eyelashes to grow inward. After two simple operations totaling less than $250, Hugo's sight was restored, he quickly caught up in school, and applied for one of our educational scholarships to become a mechanic.  He graduated, is able to support his siblings, and hopes to open his own shop.  With the help of our donors - you guys - Hugo's family is able to break free of crushing poverty.

These are just a few of the amazing things that the RVF worked on this year.  If you check out our website (RosaVeraFund.org) you will see the actual faces of the women and children we work with.  Many of the children would not be alive today without your support.  For that, we thank you!

Please consider renewing your support for the RVF this year.  We want to reach you before all the other end-of-year requests arrive.  The easiest way is to make a donation is online through our website.  You can choose to make your donation all at once, or monthly.

If you came to help us celebrate, but have not had a chance yet to learn about our work, I hope you will take a few minutes to check it out.

Unlike almost any other nonprofit out there, we can honestly say that 100% of our donations are used for the families - not one penny is taken out for administrative expenses.  You can be sure that your donation really counts.

Sincerely,

Karen
