Case studies:  Edith  María  Julio

Julio originates from a humble family that survives on the meager earnings provided by his father’s occupation of purchasing worn shoes and rehabilitating them for sale at the market.  Despite his family’s best efforts, Julio, his parents and his three younger siblings live in poverty.

Julio’s story is unique in that he approached the health center with the specific desire to learn and advance himself.  Despite having no training or knowledge of computers, he expressed clearly that computer science was precisely the field that he hoped to master. Typical of our partner organization’s character, it was decided to give this motivated young man a real chance.

Although he attended the same training seminars on health education and home visitation as other Community Health Workers do, Julio was also immersed in the organization’s computer center.  From typing to maintenance to programming, Julio quickly went from learning how to turn a computer on to being in charge of the organization’s Medical Statistics Program.  In January of 2008, Julio became the RVF’s first official recipient of a scholarship from its new Community Health Worker Educational Program.  As his first step, Julio chose to enroll in a one year practicum offered by the region’s best computer training institute.  The competency that he has gained and applies to our programs has offered real benefits to the RVF, the Consejo de Salud Rural Andino, and most importantly, to Julio and his family.
