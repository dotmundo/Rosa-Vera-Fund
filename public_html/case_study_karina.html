Case studies:  Karina  María Cielo  Mathias

Karina Becerra has been a patient of the RVF since 2006. When we first met her she was receiving dialysis because of her failing kidneys. This put a tremendous strain on her family, as they could not keep up with the mounting expense of treatment. The logistical difficulty of transportating her to a different city multiple times each week for dialysis was tremendous. Karina herself once said, “It was becoming hard for me to find the energy to wake up each day." The RVF stepped in and donations paid for her dialysis while the search for an eligible kidney donor continued. 

In July of 2010, Karina was finally able to receive a kidney from her aunt, thanks to generous donors who contributed to her transplant surgery. It is our goal to continue to support Karina in the years ahead in order for her to reach her potential. She is a straight A student and has shown much discipline and maturity throughout her life; she plans to study nursing after graduating from high school. Currently, the RVF provides her with costly medicine, follow up visits with her nephrologist, and the clean water and special foods she must take daily in order to keep her body from refecting her transplanted kidney or succumbing to infection. So far, she has been recognized as one the few successful pediatric renal transplant cases in Bolivia. 

“I Karina, thank you with all my heart.  I will not tire of thanking you for your help."
