A few notes from Karen Melk, Secretary of the Rosa Vera Fund.

On January 18th, 2011, my dear friend Joe Andriola, a strong supporter of the Rosa Vera Fund, died tragically in a car accident while traveling in China.

Joe was a very close friend of my husband Tim and mine. Joe's wife Judy Carty asked that in lieu of flowers, donations be made to the Rosa Vera Fund in Joe's honor. Many of Joe's family members, friends, colleagues and clients did just that; they honored Joe's celebration of life by making a donation that would give a child the opportunity to get better, to receive medical care, and to live a life of dignity. All of us at the Rosa Vera Fund are deeply touched and honored that Judy thought that supporting the work of our fund was a meaningful way to honor Joe.

Joe was generous, kind-hearted, keenly intelligent, adventurous, charismatic, loyal and thoughtful, and adored by all who knew him. Joe's greatest accomplishment was the love and joy he shared with those who were part of his life. He and Judy were true soulmates and Joe was a devoted, loving father to his two young children, Amelia and Michael.

There are so many wonderful things to say about Joe and his life; I would like to single out three of his strongest characteristics.

Joe was a genuine person. So many people felt close to Joe because he was so approachable and caring. Family, friends, colleagues and clients always knew they could trust Joe to be there and to get the job done. It is no surprise Joe was such a successful person, architect, and businessman.

Joe was a team player. He was always the person willing to take one for the team. Many of you have personally experienced this or read about his personal commitment to making his business successful in unprecedented times. It is ironic and sad to see that his search for success brought him to China, where he lost his life.

Joe made everything fun. Tim and I always looked forward to being with Joe because he had a way of seeing the bright side and the humor in everything. That created an aura of happiness and joy in all he did. We will miss that most.

Donations made in Joe's memory have totaled more than $13,000 to date. We plan to work with Judy to do something very significant and special with this money; several children's lives will be saved, and several families will be able to rise out of dire poverty, because of the donations from people who loved and cared about Joe.

Although Joe is no longer with us, we can all move on taking a lesson from Joe's play book – be genuine, help each other, and have fun. Joe would want us to do that.

We are all better people for having had Joe in our lives.
